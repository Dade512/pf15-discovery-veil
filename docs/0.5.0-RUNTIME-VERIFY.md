Status: **PASSED — live two-client (2026-06-16).** GM-side automation confirmed suppression,
privacy, private-store identity, GM annotation, and the broker; a follow-up **live two-browser
pass** (GM "Dade" on Vivaldi / player "DXWarlock" on Chrome) confirmed the player-seat relay end to
end (see "Two-client relay pass"). Driven via in-page evaluation on the live clients, Foundry

## What was verified

### Suppression + privacy (the riskiest surface — PASS)
- A non-player actor (NPC "Oswald", `hasPlayerOwner === false`) cast **Magic Missile**.
- **First attempt surfaced a real bug:** setting `shared.scriptData.hideChat` in `pf1PreActionUse`
  did NOT suppress the card — PF1's `executeScriptCalls()` runs after that hook and rebuilds
  `scriptData`, clobbering the flag. The real Magic Missile card leaked alongside the generic one
  (`messagesDelta = 2`, one real `type:"action"` card containing "Magic Missile").
- **Fix:** suppress at **`pf1PreDisplayActionUse` returning `false`** (fires inside `postMessage`,
  after script calls, immediately before `ChatMessage.create`), tracking masked casts in a
  `WeakMap` keyed by the live ActionUse instance (PF1 cannot clobber it).
- **Re-test PASS:** `messagesDelta = 1` (only the generic card), `realSpellCardLeak = 0`. The single
  `preCreateChatMessage` event was the generic card (`hasMM:false`); no real spell card was created.
- The generic card carries **no** spell identity: content = "A spell is being cast.", `whisper:[]`,
  flags = `{ castId, kind:"maskedSpell" }` (a random castId only). `contentHasMM:false`,
  `flagsHasMM:false`.
- Public registry entry is safe-only: `{ state:"masked", identifiedBy:{}, publicLabel }` — no name,
  school, uuid, or DC. The registry `spellcasting` JSON contains no secret.
- The true identity is stored ONLY in the client-scope private store (`privateStore` localStorage):
  `{ spellName:"Magic Missile", school:"evo", spellItemUuid, casterActorUuid, spellLevel:1, dc:16 }`.
  DC = **15 + spell level** (PF1 rule), derived at mask time.

### GM visibility (PASS)
- On the GM's client only, `renderChatMessageHTML` annotates the generic card (presentation-only,
  read from the private store — never written to the document): note rendered as
  **"GM: Magic Missile · Evocation · Spellcraft DC 16"** plus a **"Request Spellcraft"** button.

### Broker + reveal (PASS, driven via the GM socket handler)
- Drove `executeAsGM("spellcraftResult", { castId, userId, actorId, actorName:"Pants", total:20 })`.
- `20 >= DC 16` → `markIdentified` recorded **safe** attribution
  (`identifiedBy[userId] = { actorId, actorName:"Pants", round, source:"spellcraft" }`) and the gate
  stayed `masked` (personal reveal; not globalized — Decision #6).
- The reveal was a **Foundry-filtered whisper to the earner only**: content
  "You identified the spell: Magic Missile (Evocation).", `whisper:[<that user id>]`
  (`onlyToPlayer:true`). Non-identifying players never receive the document.

## Two-client relay pass (PASS — 2026-06-16, GM Vivaldi + player Chrome)
Pants was temporarily trained in Spellcraft (`spl.rank` 0→1, restored after); the rank replicated
to the player client. A non-player spell (Oswald / Magic Missile) was masked on the GM seat, then:

- **GM → player:** the rank bump and masked state replicated to the player's client correctly
  (`ownsPants:true`, `spl.rank:1`), confirming eligibility cross-client.
- **Player roll capture (player seat):** `actor.rollSkill("spl")` fired `pf1ActorRollSkill` with
  `skillId === "spl"`; the total was read from `chatMessage.rolls[0].total`.
- **Player → GM relay (cross-client):** the player called
  `executeAsGM("spellcraftResult", { castId, userId, actorId, total })`. A first total (15) vs the
  derived DC 16 correctly produced **no reveal** (`identifiedBy` empty, 0 whispers) — the fail
  branch. The hidden DC was then lowered to 10; a re-roll (total 10 ≥ 10) was relayed.
- **Broker → reveal (cross-client):** the GM broker (other browser) marked the player identified
  (safe attribution only) and created the reveal whisper. **The whisper arrived on the player's own
  seat:** "You identified the spell: Magic Missile (Evocation)." (`whisper:[<player>]`).
- **Player-seat privacy:** `playerCanSeeRealSpellCard === 0` — the player never saw a real
  Magic Missile card, only the generic notice and the reveal they earned.

All test state was restored (cards deleted from their authoring seat — player-authored Spellcraft
rolls deleted on the player seat, GM-authored cards on the GM seat; Pants rank, Oswald slot, gate,
and private identity all reverted).

## Note — non-active-GM masking is deliberately fail-secret (multi-GM)
`onPreActionUse` masks on any GM client, but `setHiddenSpellIdentity` is active-GM-only. On a
multi-GM table, a *non-active* GM casting a non-player spell will still suppress the real card and
post the generic notice (no leak), but cannot store the identity → an unrevealable mask (the GM is
warned). This is **fail-secret by design.** The correct multi-GM fix is to relay the identity to the
active GM over the socket — NOT to gate masking on `isActiveGMClient()`, which would skip suppression
and let the real card leak. For this single-GM table the active GM is the only GM, so it does not
arise. (Lyra audit 2026-06-16, finding 1.)

## Notes / by-design
- Masking withholds the bundled mechanics (attack/save/damage) with the suppressed card — the MVP
  posts the generic notice only (Decision A / name+school-first). A future iteration may re-post
  stripped public mechanics.
- Scope: non-player casters only (`hasPlayerOwner === false`). Player self-casts are out of scope.
